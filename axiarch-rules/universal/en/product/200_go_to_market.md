## Part I. GTM Philosophy & Prerequisites

### 1.1. GTM Definition & Scope

- **Rule 12.001**: Define GTM (Go-To-Market) strategy as "the comprehensive design of who, what, how, and at what price to deliver value"
- **Rule 12.002**: GTM is the product team's responsibility. Siloing it as "marketing's job" is prohibited
- **The 4 GTM Elements**:

```
Who:   Who to reach (ICP / Persona)
What:  What to deliver (Value Proposition / Positioning)
How:   How to deliver (Channel / GTM Motion)
When:  Timing in the launch sequence
```

### 1.2. GTM Prerequisites

- **Rule 12.003**: Do not begin significant GTM investment until all of the following are confirmed

```
Prerequisites:
□ Problem-Solution Fit confirmed (Customer Discovery from §100_market_validation complete)
□ ICP is concretized with 3+ real customers
□ Core value proposition can be stated in one sentence
□ Clear differentiation from competitors is defined
□ Basic product delivery capability (MVP or above) exists
```

### 1.3. GTM ≠ Marketing Campaign

- **Rule 12.004**: Do not conflate GTM strategy with "running ads." It is a comprehensive design including:
  - Pricing design
  - Sales channel construction
  - Sales process design
  - Customer success preparation
  - Onboarding design

### 1.4. Non-Linear Buyer Journey

- **Rule 12.005**: Buyers in 2026 do not follow a linear funnel. **70-90% of purchase research occurs in the Dark Funnel** (channels invisible to sales)

```
Traditional Funnel Model (✘ Deprecated):
Awareness → Interest → Decision → Action

2026 Buyer Journey (✔ Adopted):
Buyers cycle non-linearly through:
- Self-Education (Review sites, communities, peer recommendations)
- Self-Serve Trial (Free plans, demo environments)
- Peer Validation (G2/Capterra/Slack communities)
- Vendor Engagement (Only 10-30% of the total journey)
```

- **Rule 12.006**: In GTM design, assume that "buyers have completed the majority of their decision-making before contacting sales." Mandate the following:
  - Make digital assets (website, content, pricing page) function as the "primary sales team"
  - Enhance self-serve experiences (free trials, demos, interactive content)
  - Strategically manage third-party validation (G2, Capterra, reviews, case studies)

- **Rule 12.006a**: **Trust-First Discovery** — The flood of AI-generated content (AI Slop) has materialized a "trust crisis" among buyers. The following are mandatory
  - **Human Authenticity Signal**: All content must include real author/expert signatures and profiles (publishing anonymous AI-generated content is prohibited)
  - **Proof, Not Persuasion**: Provide case studies, quantitative data, and third-party validation at every funnel stage. Prioritize evidence (Proof) over persuasion
  - **Interactive Demo / Sandbox**: Provide interactive demos that buyers can operate themselves, rather than static screenshots or videos (Arcade / Navattic / Storylane, etc.)
  - **Transparent AI Disclosure**: Explicitly disclose AI involvement in content, recommendations, and chat

### 1.5. Buyer-Led Growth (BLG) Paradigm

- **Rule 12.007**: 2026 GTM is founded on the **Buyer-Led Growth (BLG)** paradigm. Shift from seller-controlled funnel management to a design that supports the buyer's autonomous purchasing process

```
BLG 3 Principles:

1. Facilitate, Don't Control
   - Do not attempt to control the buyer's research, comparison, and evaluation process
   - Provide necessary information, tools, and evidence at the timing buyers seek them
   - Minimize gated content and prioritize open access

2. Enable Self-Qualification
   - Provide ROI Calculator, TCO comparison tools, and interactive demos
   - Build an environment where buyers can autonomously determine fit
   - Design digital experiences where 80% of value is understood before sales contact

3. Support the Buying Committee
   - B2B purchase decisions involve an average of 6-10 stakeholders
   - Prepare role-specific content (Champion, Decision Maker, Technical Evaluator, Procurement)
   - Provide internal approval document templates to support Champion's internal advocacy
```

- **Rule 12.008**: BLG KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|:----|:----|:-----|
| **Self-Serve Evaluation Rate** | Percentage of leads completing self-serve evaluation before sales contact | ≥ 60% |
| **Content-to-Pipeline Ratio** | Conversion rate from content engagement to pipeline | ≥ 5% |
| **Buying Committee Coverage** | Number of stakeholders contacted per deal | ≥ 4 |
| **Ungated Content Engagement** | Engagement rate of open content | ≥ 3.0x vs gated |

## Part II. Positioning Strategy

### 2.1. Positioning Principles

- **Rule 12.010**: Define positioning not as "what we are" but as **"the position occupied in the customer's mind"** (Al Ries & Jack Trout)
- **Rule 12.011**: Aim not to compare against competitors but to **create a new category** within the customer's existing mental framework

### 2.2. April Dunford's Positioning Canvas

- **Rule 12.012**: Define positioning using these 5 elements ([Obviously Awesome, April Dunford])

| Element | Question | Example |
|:--------|:---------|:--------|
| **Competitive Alternatives** | What would customers use without you? | Excel, email, nothing |
| **Unique Attributes** | What do you have that others don't? | Real-time sync, AI suggestion |
| **Value for Customer** | What value does that attribute deliver? | 60% reduction in approval time |
| **Target Customer** | Who benefits most? | Finance managers with 10+ monthly approvals |
| **Market Category** | How should you be categorized? | "AI Financial Approval Platform" |

### 2.3. Category Creation Strategy

- **Rule 12.013**: Entering existing categories risks price competition. Explore creating a new category when:
  - Existing categories don't accurately describe the customer's need
  - A new problem definition can make competitors "incomparable"
  - You can establish the category definition before industry peers

### 2.4. Messaging Hierarchy

- **Rule 12.014**: Organize external messaging in 3 layers

```
Layer 1: Tagline / Headline (Capture attention in 3 seconds)
         Example: "Expense reports done in 5 minutes"

Layer 2: Sub-headline / Value Proposition (Convey value in 30 seconds)
         Example: "Just scan your receipt. Automatic categorization frees you from end-of-month expense nightmares."

Layer 3: Feature Proof Points (Demonstrate evidence in 3 minutes)
         Example: 99.5% accuracy AI OCR / Real-time sync / ...
```

### 2.5. Narrative Design (Storytelling Strategy)

- **Rule 12.015**: Define Narrative Design to deliver positioning as a "story"

```
Narrative Design Structure:

1. Old World: Describe the current problem in empathetic language
   Example: "Expense reports are an end-of-month nightmare. Hunting for receipts, manual entry, waiting for approval."

2. Catalyst: Why change now?
   Example: "With AI OCR, automating expense reports is finally realistic."

3. New World: The future your product delivers
   Example: "Just snap a photo. AI auto-categorizes and completes the approval flow."

4. Proof: Back it up with evidence and data
   Example: "Company X reduced expense processing time by 60%. Zero overtime at month-end."
```

- **Rule 12.016**: Use the Narrative consistently across all channels. Website, sales deck, case studies, and ad creatives must all derive from the same Narrative

## Part III. ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) Definition

### 3.1. ICP vs Persona

- **Rule 12.020**: Clearly distinguish between ICP and Persona

| Concept | Level | Purpose |
|:--------|:------|:--------|
| **ICP** | Organization (B2B) / Segment (B2C) | Strategic target selection |
| **Persona** | Individual | UI design and copywriting |

### 3.2. B2B ICP Definition

- **Rule 12.021**: Define B2B ICP with the following attributes

```yaml
b2b_icp:
  firmographics:
    industry: ["SaaS", "Fintech"]
    employees: "50-500"
    revenue: "$5M–$50M"
    geography: ["US", "UK", "EU"]
    tech_stack: ["Salesforce", "Slack", "AWS"]
  
  situational:
    trigger_events:
      - "Post-Series A scaling phase"
      - "Sales team exceeds 10 people"
      - "90 days before existing tool contract renewal"
    pain_indicators:
      - "3+ sales job openings on LinkedIn"
      - "Active CRO hiring post"
    disqualifiers:
      - "Fewer than 10 employees"
      - "Under long-term contract with a competitor"
```

### 3.3. B2C ICP Definition

- **Rule 12.022**: Define B2C ICP based on behavior and situation (demographics are supplementary)

```yaml
b2c_icp:
  behavioral:
    trigger: "Currently paying $20+/month for an alternative solution"
    frequency: "Faces this problem 3+ times per week"
    digital_savvy: "Has 10+ apps installed on smartphone"
  
  attitudinal:
    pain_awareness: "Clearly aware of the problem (Pain-Aware)"
    solution_awareness: "Knows solutions exist"
    urgency: "Wants to solve within the next 90 days"
```

### 3.4. ICP Scoring Model

- **Rule 12.023**: Build an ICP match scoring system to improve sales and marketing efficiency

```
ICP Score = Σ(Attribute Match × Weight)

Score ≥ 80: Tier 1 (Priority) → AE direct contact
Score 60-79: Tier 2 → SDR + sequence
Score 40-59: Tier 3 → Marketing nurture
Score < 40: Disqualified → Auto-remove
```

## Part IV. GTM Motion Selection

### 4.1. GTM Motion Types

- **Rule 12.030**: Select from the following 4 GTM motions (overlap allowed); clearly define the primary motion

| Motion | Description | Best Fit |
|:-------|:-----------|:---------|
| **PLG** (Product-Led Growth) | Product itself is the primary acquisition/expansion engine | Viral SaaS, consumer products |
| **SLG** (Sales-Led Growth) | Sales team is the primary acquisition engine | Enterprise, high-ACV B2B |
| **MLG** (Marketing-Led Growth) | Content / brand is the primary acquisition engine | Education, complex decision SaaS |
| **CLG** (Community-Led Growth) | Community is the primary acquisition/expansion engine | DevTools, OSS, niche professionals |
| **ELG** (Event-Led Growth) | Events / conferences are the primary acquisition engine | Enterprise, industry-specific SaaS |

### 4.2. PLG Implementation Protocol

- **Rule 12.031**: When adopting PLG, design the following 3 engines

```
3 PLG Engines:
1. Acquisition Engine
   - Freemium / free trial WTP threshold design
   - Viral loop embedding (sharing, invitations, embeds)
   - App Store / Product Hunt optimization

2. Activation Engine
   - Time-to-Value (first value experience) ≤ 5 minutes
   - Minimize steps to "Aha Moment"

3. Expansion Engine
   - Seat expansion / department spread design
   - Usage-based billing alignment (more use = more value)
```

- **Rule 12.031a**: **Reverse Trial / Credit-Based Model** — For AI-Native products or products with high compute costs (COGS), traditional unlimited freemium is unsustainable. Consider the following models

```
PLG Onboarding Evolution:

1. Reverse Trial
   - Provide full-feature access (14-30 days)
   - Choose free plan or paid plan after expiration
   - Improve paid conversion by "letting users experience value first"
   - Target: Reverse Trial → Paid Conversion ≥ 25%

2. Credit-Based Model
   - Grant initial free credits (e.g., $50 worth of AI processing)
   - Usage-based billing based on credit consumption
   - Directly reflects AI COGS, solving the freemium cost problem
   - Target: Credit Utilization Rate ≥ 70%

3. Work-as-a-Service (Task-Based Pricing)
   - Charge by "completed tasks" rather than "seats"
   - Suitable when AI Agents replace human work
   - Customers pay for outcomes, not access
```

### 4.3. SLG Implementation Protocol

- **Rule 12.032**: When adopting SLG, design and document each stage

```
SLG Basic Structure:
Lead → MQL → SQL → SAL → Opportunity → Closed Won

Define in Blueprint:
- MQL definition criteria
- SQL definition criteria
- BANT / MEDDIC qualification criteria
```

### 4.4. GTM Motion Transition Protocol

- **Rule 12.033**: Consider transitioning from PLG to SLG (Product-Led Sales) when:
  - 5+ inbound enterprise inquiries per month
  - Organic expansion from free plan begins stalling
  - Customers with ACV > $10K appear

### 4.5. Product-Led Sales (PLS) Hybrid Protocol

- **Rule 12.034**: Do not treat PLG and SLG as mutually exclusive. Design a **Product-Led Sales (PLS) hybrid model** where sales upsells and expands users acquired via PLG

```
PLS Basic Structure:

PLG → PQA (Product Qualified Account) → PQL (Product Qualified Lead) → AE Contact

PQA Definition (Account-level signals):
  - 3+ active users from the same domain
  - Approaching free plan limits (storage, seats, etc.)
  - Viewing Enterprise-specific feature pages (SSO, audit logs, etc.)

PQL Definition (Individual-level signals):
  - Aha Moment achieved (Activation Metric met)
  - 3+ consecutive days of login
  - Used team invite functionality
  - Visited pricing page 2+ times
```

- **Rule 12.035**: PQL handoff to sales must be a **Signal-Based Handoff** with the following automated
  - Auto-create CRM record when PQL score exceeds threshold
  - Instant notification to assigned AE via Slack / Teams
  - AE must make first contact **within 24 hours** (Speed-to-Lead)

- **Rule 12.036**: Define PLS KPIs as follows

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|:----|:-----------|:-------|
| **PQL Conversion Rate** | PQL to Opportunity conversion rate | ≥ 15% |
| **PQL-to-Close Cycle** | Days from PQL to closed won | ≤ 21 days (50% shorter than SLG) |
| **Self-Serve Revenue %** | Self-serve share of total revenue | 30-50% |
| **Expansion Revenue via PLS** | Expansion revenue driven by PLS | 20%+ contribution to NRR |

### 4.6. GTM Motion Maturity Model

- **Rule 12.037**: Select appropriate GTM motion composition based on ARR phase

| ARR Phase | Recommended Motion | Investment Allocation | Focus |
|:----------|:------------------|:---------------------|:------|
| **$0–$1M** | Founder-Led Sales + PLG | S&M 10-20% of Burn | PMF validation, first 10 customers |
| **$1M–$5M** | PLG + SDR/AE (2-3) | S&M 30-40% of Revenue | Channel discovery, Playbook creation |
| **$5M–$20M** | PLS Hybrid + MLG | S&M 40-50% of Revenue | Channel concentration, segment expansion |
| **$20M–$100M** | PLS + ABM + Partner | S&M 30-40% of Revenue | Multi-motion optimization |
| **$100M+** | All motions + Category Creation | S&M 25-35% of Revenue | Market dominance, international expansion |

- **Rule 12.038**: Conduct a mandatory **GTM Motion Review** at each phase transition, evaluating:
  - Whether current motion's CAC Payback is within 12 months
  - Whether channel-level LTV/CAC ratio maintains ≥ 3.0
  - Whether sales productivity (AE Quota attainment) is ≥ 80%
  - Whether hiring plan for next-phase capabilities (PMM, Growth Eng, Partner Mgr) is established

### 4.7. Ecosystem-Led Growth (ELG) & Nearbound Protocol

- **Rule 12.039**: In addition to PLG and SLG, design **Ecosystem-Led Growth (ELG)** as the third growth engine. ELG leverages trust and existing relationships within the partner ecosystem to **convert cold outreach into warm introductions**

```
ELG Basic Structure:

Partner Ecosystem → Account Mapping → EQL Generation → Co-Sell / Warm Intro → Closed Won

EQL (Ecosystem-Qualified Lead) Definition:
  - Target accounts where a partner already has an established trust relationship
  - Identified via Account Mapping tools (Crossbeam / Reveal etc.)
  - Leads accompanied by partner-sourced referral or recommendation

Nearbound Selling in Practice:
  - Transform "cold" outreach into "warm" via partner leverage
  - Identify partners with existing relationships to target accounts
  - Partner-facilitated introductions and joint proposals
```

- **Rule 12.039a**: ELG KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|:----|:----|:-----|
| **EQL Conversion Rate** | EQL to Opportunity conversion rate | ≥ 25% (3x+ vs cold leads) |
| **Partner-Influenced Revenue** | ARR share of deals with partner involvement | ≥ 30% of total ARR |
| **Account Mapping Coverage** | Partner coverage of target accounts | ≥ 60% |
| **Nearbound Win Rate** | Close rate of partner-referred deals | ≥ 2.0x vs non-partner |

- **Rule 12.039b**: ELG Prerequisites
  - ≥ 5 active partners in the partner ecosystem
  - Account Mapping tool adopted and operational
  - **Monthly Account Mapping Review** established with partners
  - Partner incentive design (referral fees / Co-Sell margins) defined

### 4.8. Community-Led Growth (CLG) Implementation Protocol

- **Rule 12.039c**: When adopting CLG, design the community as a **"Product Growth Engine"**, not a "marketing channel." Unilateral promotional content dumping into the community is prohibited

```
CLG Flywheel Structure:

1. Value-First Contribution
   - Provide value before asking for engagement (education, tools, templates)
   - Prioritize "problem solving" over "pitching"
   - Build an environment where members organically recommend the product

2. Peer Learning Network
   - Facilitate best-practice sharing among users
   - Officially recognize power users as Champions / Ambassadors
   - Elevate Community-Generated Content (UGC) to official documentation

3. Product Feedback Loop
   - Connect community feature requests directly to the Product Board
   - Offer beta features as Community Early Access
   - Announce implemented features as "community-originated"

4. Advocacy Engine
   - Identify and nurture NPS 9-10 members
   - Integrate with referral programs
   - Enable case study creation, speaking opportunities, and co-created content
```

- **Rule 12.039d**: CLG Channel Design

| Channel | Purpose | Product Type Fit |
|:--------|:------|:--------------|
| **Slack / Discord** | Real-time Q&A, peer support | DevTools, SaaS |
| **GitHub Discussions** | Technical discussion, RFCs | OSS, API products |
| **Forum (Discourse etc.)** | Long-term knowledge accumulation, SEO asset | Complex domain products |
| **User Group / Meetup** | In-person networking, deep relationship building | Enterprise, industry-specific |
| **Ambassador Program** | Scalable word-of-mouth | All types |

- **Rule 12.039e**: CLG KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|:----|:----|:-----|
| **Community-Sourced Leads** | Leads generated through community | ≥ 15% of total leads |
| **Community DAU/MAU** | Community daily/monthly active ratio | DAU/MAU ≥ 30% |
| **Support Deflection** | Percentage of support issues resolved within community | ≥ 25% |
| **Community NPS** | NPS of community members | ≥ 60 |
| **UGC Contribution Rate** | Member content contribution rate | Monthly ≥ 5% of members |

### 4.9. Customer-Led Growth (CuLG) Protocol

- **Rule 12.039f**: **Customer-Led Growth (CuLG)** differs from CLG (community-driven) and designs **existing customer usage data, telemetry, and feedback loops** as the core growth engine

```
CuLG Framework:

1. Usage Intelligence
   - Collect and analyze product usage data in real-time
   - Identify high-engagement patterns → Convert to Expansion triggers
   - Detect low engagement → Activate Churn Prevention

2. Customer Feedback as GTM Input
   - Reflect NPS / CSAT / CES data directly in GTM messaging
   - Systematically convert customer success stories into GTM assets (Case Study Pipeline)
   - Product Roadmap → GTM Narrative alignment design

3. Expansion-First Revenue
   - Position NRR as the most critical GTM metric
   - Prioritize Expansion CAC efficiency over new acquisition CAC
   - Design customer referrals as the primary channel

4. Post-Purchase GTM
   - Design 4 stages: Onboarding → Adoption → Expansion → Advocacy
   - Automate "next best action" at each stage
   - Link Customer Health Score with GTM actions
```

- **Rule 12.039g**: CuLG Applicability Conditions
  - Full deployment when NRR accounts for **≥ 30%** of revenue growth
  - Customer Health Score infrastructure must be established
  - Product Usage Analytics (Amplitude / Mixpanel / Pendo, etc.) must be operational

- **Rule 12.039h**: CuLG KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|:----|:----|:-----|
| **NRR** | Existing customer revenue retention/expansion rate | ≥ 115% |
| **Expansion Revenue Ratio** | Expansion revenue as % of total revenue | ≥ 35% |
| **Customer-Sourced Pipeline** | Pipeline ratio sourced from existing customers | ≥ 25% |
| **Time-to-Expand** | Average days from initial to expansion contract | ≤ 120 days |
| **Referral Conversion Rate** | Conversion rate from customer referrals | ≥ 30% |

### 4.10. Vertical GTM & Micro-Market Strategy

- **Rule 12.039i**: **Vertical GTM** rejects generic messaging and designs GTM tailored to industry-specific workflows, regulations, and challenges. In 2026, the effectiveness of generic positioning is declining rapidly

```
Vertical GTM Design Framework:

1. Vertical Selection
   Selection Criteria:
   □ Industries where product value is most apparent
   □ Fit with industry-specific regulatory and compliance requirements
   □ Referral density within the industry (likelihood of peer recommendations)
   □ High TAM/SAM ratio warranting concentrated investment

2. Vertical Playbook
   □ Articulate industry-specific challenges and pain points
   □ Messaging in industry terminology (eliminate generic expressions)
   □ Industry-specific case studies and ROI results
   □ Industry-specific integration partners (EHR, ERP, vertical SaaS, etc.)

3. Micro-Market Pilot
   □ Start with 50-100 company pilots when entering new verticals
   □ Validate messaging, pricing, and onboarding at small scale
   □ Go/No-Go decision based on pilot results (within 30 days)
   □ Win Rate ≥ 25% as threshold for full-scale rollout
```

- **Rule 12.039j**: Vertical GTM KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|:----|:----|:-----|
| **Vertical Win Rate** | Win rate for vertical-specific deals | ≥ 1.5x vs generic |
| **Vertical ACV** | Average ACV for vertical-specific deals | ≥ 1.3x vs generic |
| **Industry NPS** | NPS by industry vertical | ≥ 55 |
| **Vertical Referral Rate** | Intra-industry referral rate | ≥ 20% |

### 4.11. GTM Motion Compounding Loop Design

- **Rule 12.039k**: Beyond optimizing individual GTM motions, design **Compounding Loops (compound interest synergies) between motions**. Top companies in 2026 build circular structures where each motion fuels the next

```
GTM Compounding Loop Structure:

PLG → PLS → ELG → CLG → CuLG → PLG (circular)

1. PLG → PLS (Product Usage → Sales Expansion)
   - Free user usage data generates PQL signals
   - AEs execute upsell/cross-sell based on PQLs
   - Flywheel: Product improvement → Usage ↑ → PQL ↑ → Revenue ↑

2. PLS → ELG (Sales Close → Ecosystem Expansion)
   - Closed customers adopt integration partners → Co-Sell deal creation
   - Success stories incorporated into partner sales materials
   - Flywheel: Customer success → Partner trust → EQL ↑

3. ELG → CLG (Ecosystem → Community Expansion)
   - Partner-sourced users join community
   - Knowledge sharing in community reduces support load
   - Flywheel: Members ↑ → UGC ↑ → SEO/AEO impact → New inflow

4. CLG → CuLG (Community → Customer-Led Growth)
   - Active community members become Champions
   - Champions drive internal rollout → Expansion Revenue
   - Flywheel: Champion activity → NRR ↑ → Case studies ↑ → PLG material

5. CuLG → PLG (Customer Success → Product Improvement)
   - Customer feedback and usage data feeds product roadmap
   - Product improvements shorten TTV for new users
   - Flywheel: Customer voice → Feature improvement → Experience ↑ → Viral ↑
```

- **Rule 12.039l**: Compounding Loop Design Guardrails
  - Do not optimize all motions simultaneously. **Focus on 2-3 motion connections based on ARR phase (§4.6)**
  - Define **Handoff Metrics** at each loop connection point and measure loop health monthly
  - If a loop breakage is detected (any motion not functioning), prioritize repairing the breakage point

- **Rule 12.039m**: Compounding Loop KPIs

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|:----|:----|:-----|
| **Loop Cycle Time** | Average time for 1 cycle (PLG→PLG) | ≤ 180 days |
| **Cross-Motion Conversion** | Inter-motion handoff success rate | ≥ 40% |
| **Compounding Revenue Ratio** | Revenue ratio involving 2+ motions | ≥ 30% |

## Part V. Channel Strategy

### 5.1. Channel Discovery Protocol

- **Rule 12.040**: Run 3–5 parallel channel experiments initially, then **concentrate resources on 1 channel** within 4–8 weeks
- **Rule 12.041**: Evaluate each channel using the following criteria

### 5.2. Channel Characteristics

- **Rule 12.042**: Prioritize **concentration over diversification** in acquisition channels. Do not pursue the next channel until CAC recovery is established in one
- **Rule 12.043**: "Channel diversification" is a dependency risk management activity—target ARR > $1M before pursuing it

### 5.4. Partner-Led Growth Protocol

### 5.6. GTM Compliance & Privacy-First Outreach

- **Rule 12.049a**: All GTM outreach activities MUST **fully comply** with the following regulations. Violations lead to fines, domain blacklisting, and brand damage

```
Region-Specific Cold Outreach Regulations:

United States (CAN-SPAM Act):
  Model: Opt-out (no prior consent required)
  Requirements:
  □ Valid physical postal address included
  □ Clear and functional opt-out mechanism
  □ Honest subject lines and accurate sender information
  □ Honor opt-out requests within 10 business days
  Penalty: $50,000+ per email violation

European Union (GDPR):
  Model: B2C requires consent / B2B uses "Legitimate Interest"
  Requirements:
  □ Document Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA)
  □ Outreach must be highly relevant to recipient's professional role
  □ Immediate opt-out compliance
  Penalty: 4% of global annual revenue or €20M, whichever is higher

Canada (CASL):
  Model: Strict Opt-in (prior consent required)
  □ Maintain verifiable records of express or implied consent

Japan (Act on Regulation of Transmission of Specified Electronic Mail):
  Model: Opt-in (prior consent required)
  □ Sender's name, address, and contact information clearly stated
  □ Unsubscribe method clearly stated
  □ Consent acquisition records preserved
```

- **Rule 12.049b**: Email Technical Requirements (Non-Negotiable)
  - **SPF / DKIM / DMARC** records are mandatory. Emails without them are auto-rejected
  - **Domain Separation**: Send cold outreach from a **dedicated sending domain** only (sending from primary domain is prohibited)
  - **Volume Limits**: Cap at **30-50 emails/day** per inbox
  - **Bounce Rate**: Verify lists and maintain bounce rate **below 2%**

- **Rule 12.049c**: Privacy-First Marketing Principles
  - **Open Rate is Unreliable**: Due to Apple Mail Privacy Protection etc., exclude Open Rate from KPIs. Measure Reply Rate, Click Rate, and Meeting Conversion instead
  - **Zero-Party Data Priority**: Prioritize consent-based data collection via owned channels over purchasing third-party data
  - **AI-Generated Outreach**: Even when using AI for content generation, final output must maintain **personalized, human-quality** standards

## Part VI. Launch Plan Design

### 6.1. Three-Phase Launch Structure

- **Rule 12.050**: Design every product launch in the following 3 phases

```
Phase 1: Soft Launch (Limited access)
Goal: Real feedback from early adopters
Target: Top of waitlist / trusted existing network
KPI: NPS ≥ 30, Day-7 Retention ≥ 30%

Phase 2: Closed Beta (Invite-only)
Goal: Strengthen PMF signals / Squash bugs / Collect testimonials
Target: 50–200 ICP-matching users
KPI: Sean Ellis Test ≥ 40%, Churn < 10%/month

Phase 3: Public Launch
Goal: Maximum awareness / Channel validation
Target: Public
KPI: Web traffic, Signups, Conversion, MRR
```

### 6.2. Product Hunt Launch Protocol

- **Rule 12.051**: When using Product Hunt, execute the following

```
Pre-Launch (4 weeks before):
□ Build up Hunter / Maker profiles
□ Contribute to the PH community (comments / upvotes)
□ Build awareness with teaser page / waitlist
□ Build supporter list (200+ people who will upvote)

Launch Day:
□ Go live at 12:01 AM PST (use the full day)
□ Blast across all social channels (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Slack communities)
□ Upvote velocity in the first 2 hours determines Top 5 entry

Post-Launch:
□ Reply to all comments within 24 hours
□ Add acquired users to Soft Launch list
□ Publish follow-up metrics 1 month later
```

### 6.3. Launch Timing Principles

- **Rule 12.052**: Optimize launch timing based on the following

| Factor | Best Practice |
|:-------|:-------------|

### 6.7. Proof of Value (PoV) Protocol

- **Rule 12.059a**: For deals with ACV $30K+, or AI-Native products, provide a **Proof of Value (PoV)** in addition to standard demos. A PoV is a **mini-implementation** that demonstrates product value using the customer's own data and workflows

```

  □ Quantitatively evaluate Success Criteria attainment
  □ Present ROI / TCO analysis
  □ Create executive-facing results report
```

### 6.8. GTM Experimentation Framework

## Part VII. Initial Customer Acquisition Protocol


### 7.4. Land & Expand Protocol

```
  □ Seat utilization ≥ 80%
```

| **Magic Number** | Net New ARR × 4 / Prior Quarter S&M Spend | ≥ 0.7 | < 0.5 |
| **GTM Burn Ratio** | S&M Spend / Net New ARR | ≤ 1.5 | > 3.0 |
| **Sales Efficiency** | Net New ARR / AE Headcount | ≥ $250K/AE | < $150K/AE |

- **Rule 12.077**: If all metrics on the GTM Efficiency Scorecard fall into the "Warning" zone, trigger a **GTM Emergency Review** to evaluate:

  - Pricing re-verification (see `400_pricing_strategy`)

### 8.6. Capital Efficiency & SaaS Triangle

- **Rule 12.077a**: Triangulate GTM investment efficiency with the **SaaS Triangle** of 3 metrics. Pursuing any single metric in isolation is prohibited

```

1. Net Revenue Retention (NRR)

   - Target: ≥ 110% (Enterprise: ≥ 115%)

2. CAC Payback Period

3. Gross Margin
```

## Part XIII. GTM Maturity Model

- **Rule 12.098**: Evaluate GTM process maturity on a 5-level scale and progressively improve

| Level | Name | Characteristics | Key Metrics |
|:------|:----|:----|:--------|
| **L1: Ad-hoc** | Ad-hoc | Founder-dependent, no repeatability, ad-hoc sales | Customers ≤ 10 |
| **L2: Defined** | Defined | ICP defined, Sales Playbook, 1 channel established | CAC/LTV measurement begins |
| **L3: Repeatable** | Repeatable | Multi-channel, PLS hybrid, ABM adopted | Magic Number ≥ 0.7 |
| **L4: Optimized** | Optimized | AI orchestration, DSR, Signal-Based GTM | All GTM Efficiency metrics healthy |
| **L5: Predictive** | Predictive | AI prediction-based pipeline management, Agentic GTM | Forecast accuracy ≥ 90% |

- **Rule 12.099**: L3 → L4 Transition Requirements
  - All GTM Efficiency Scorecard metrics maintain "healthy line"
  - Intent Data Platform adoption and operation initiated
  - AI Sales Copilot AE utilization rate ≥ 50%

- **Rule 12.100**: L4 → L5 Transition Requirements
  - AI GTM Orchestration L3+ operational
  - Forecast accuracy (prediction vs. actual variance) ≤ 15%
  - Agentic GTM (AI Agent autonomous lead handling) PoC completed
