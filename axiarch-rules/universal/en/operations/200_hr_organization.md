## Part I. HR Philosophy & Priorities

### 1.1. Maximizing Talent Density

- **Rule 52.001**: The goal of hiring is not "increasing headcount" but **"raising the organization's Talent Density"** (Netflix)
- **Rule 52.002**: Compromising hiring standards to "fill a seat quickly" is prohibited. Do not lower the bar even when a role is vacant
- **Rule 52.003**: Define quantitative Talent Density metrics and measure quarterly

```
Talent Density Metrics (examples):
- High Performer Ratio: % of employees rated in top 20% → Target ≥ 30%
- Regrettable Attrition Rate: Turnover rate of employees you'd fight to keep → Target < 5%
- Keeper Test Pass Rate: % where answer to "Would you fight to keep them?" is Yes → Target ≥ 90%
- Internal Promotion Rate: % of promotions filled internally → Target ≥ 40%
```

### 1.2. Hire for Attitude, Train for Skills

- **Rule 52.004**: Make hiring decisions in the following priority order

```
Priority (highest to lowest):
1. Values Alignment (alignment with company values)
2. Learning Agility (speed of learning / growth mindset)
3. Attitude (ownership, integrity, drive)
4. Relevant Skills (current technical / domain proficiency)

Note: Hiring for #4 alone is prohibited.
      Without #1–3, skills alone are not a competitive advantage.
```

### 1.3. Bar-Raising Protocol

- **Rule 52.005**: For every candidate, ask: **"Will this person raise the bar above our current average?"** (Amazon Bar Raiser)
- **Rule 52.006**: Designate at least one "Bar Raiser" per hiring loop (recommended at Series A+)
  - Bar Raisers must be from outside the hiring team's direct reporting chain with veto power
  - Bar Raiser feedback must be referenced in the final debrief

### 1.4. Keeper Test

- **Rule 52.007**: All managers conduct the Keeper Test for each team member bi-annually

```
Keeper Test:
"If this team member said they were leaving, would you fight hard to keep them?"

Assessment:
- YES → Verify they receive adequate compensation and growth opportunities
- NO → Immediately begin a performance improvement conversation (PIP: §9.7)
- UNSURE → Create a feedback plan to reach clarity within 1 month

Note: The Keeper Test is a diagnostic tool for organizational health, not a termination tool
```

## Part II. Employer Branding & EVP

### 2.1. EVP (Employer Value Proposition) Design

- **Rule 52.010**: Define the EVP before beginning any recruiting activities

```
5 Pillars of EVP:
1. Compensation: Market-competitive total rewards package
2. Career: Growth opportunities, learning environment, promotion paths
3. Culture: How the team works, values, psychological safety
4. Community: Team quality, diversity, sense of belonging
5. Cause: Company mission and social impact

Definition Process:
- Conduct EVP surveys with current employees to understand "why they work here"
- Identify differentiation points vs. competitor EVPs
- Use the EVP as the unified foundation for all job postings and recruiting marketing
```

### 2.2. Recruiting Marketing

- **Rule 52.011**: Design recruiting as a marketing funnel

```
Recruiting Marketing Funnel:
Awareness
  → Tech blog, OSS contributions, conference talks, social media
Consideration
  → Company info sessions, casual chats, employee interview articles
Interest
  → Specific JDs, team introductions, project showcases
Application
  → Smooth application flow (3 steps or fewer)
```

- **Rule 52.012**: Publish tech blog posts at least twice monthly (recommended)
- **Rule 52.013**: Include OSS contributions and conference talks in engineering performance criteria

### 2.3. Candidate Experience

- **Rule 52.014**: Provide a consistently high-quality experience for all candidates

```
Candidate Experience Standards:
- Application acknowledgment: Automated reply within 24 hours
- Stage result notification: Within 3 business days of each step
- Rejection notification: Always send (ghosting is strictly prohibited)
- Rejection feedback: Provide feedback where possible
- Candidate NPS: Measure quarterly; target ≥ 60
```

- **Rule 52.015**: Educate all hiring stakeholders that rejected candidates' impressions directly impact future hiring and brand

## Part III. Job Design

### 3.1. Job Description Quality Standards

- **Rule 52.020**: Write job descriptions that enable candidates to determine "Is this role for me?"

```
Required JD Sections:
1. Why This Role (why this role exists)
2. What You'll Do (specific responsibilities)
3. What Success Looks Like (90-day and 1-year success definitions)
4. What We're Looking For (Must / Nice-to-have separated)
5. What You'll Get (compensation, equity, benefits)
6. Our Culture (why candidates should work here)

Prohibited:
❌ "Other duties as assigned" language
❌ Feature lists only (candidates can't understand the Why)
❌ Excessive requirements (a seed startup requiring 10+ years experience)
❌ Degree requirements (prioritize Skills-based Hiring)
```

### 3.2. Skills-based Hiring

- **Rule 52.021**: Do not require academic credentials (university name, degree) as mandatory hiring criteria
  - Evaluate based on **demonstrable skills and outcomes** instead
  - Prioritize portfolios, OSS contributions, and practical assessments
  - Prohibit requirements like "Bachelor's degree from top university"

### 3.3. Role Clarification

- **Rule 52.022**: Before opening any new position, document the following

```yaml
role_definition:
  mission: "Mission this role must fulfill (1 sentence)"
  key_results:
    - "What to achieve in 3 months"
    - "What to achieve in 1 year"
  decision_rights: "What this role can decide independently / needs consultation"
  collaboration:
    - "Who they'll work most closely with"
  growth_path: "Next career step from this role"
  level: "IC1-IC6 / M1-M4 (see §11 for leveling)"
```

### 3.4. Internal Mobility

- **Rule 52.023**: Encourage internal mobility to prevent external brain drain

```
Internal Mobility Policy:
- Employees with 12+ months in current role can apply for internal postings
- Internal candidates undergo the same evaluation as external (no special treatment)
- Managers are prohibited from blocking transfers (Talent Hoarding Ban)
- Knowledge transfer period upon transfer: up to 4 weeks
```

## Part IV. Compensation & Total Rewards

### 4.1. Compensation Philosophy

- **Rule 52.030**: Define compensation philosophy as a transparent set of principles

```
5 Principles of Compensation:
1. Market Competitiveness: Provide base salary at P75 (top 25%) or above
2. Internal Equity: Minimize pay variance for the same role and level
3. Pay Transparency: Publish salary ranges to all employees
4. Total Rewards: Design as a holistic package: Base + Equity + Benefits
5. Performance-linked: Variable compensation is tied to outcomes with transparent criteria
```

### 4.2. Salary Bands & Leveling

- **Rule 52.031**: Establish salary bands for all roles and levels

```
Salary Band Design Principles:
- Band Width: Define Min-Mid-Max for each level (typically ±20%)
- Overlap: Allow 10-20% overlap between adjacent levels
- New Hire Placement: Position at 40-60% of the band (near midpoint)
- Promotion: Auto-adjust to at least the next level's minimum
- Compa-Ratio: Track Individual Salary / Band Mid as a management metric (target: 0.9-1.1)

Updates:
- Update bands at least annually based on market data
- Data Sources: Radford, Pave, Levels.fyi, Option Impact, etc.
```

### 4.3. Equity Design

- **Rule 52.032**: Stock option / RSU design follows these principles

```
Equity Design Protocol:
- Vesting Schedule: 4-year / 1-year cliff (standard)
- 409A Valuation: Independent third-party valuation at least annually (IRC §409A compliant)
- Grant Sizing: Set level-based Equity Bands (prohibit arbitrary grants)
- Dilution Management: Set cap on option pool as % of total outstanding (typically 10-20%)
- Double Trigger: Recommend Double Trigger for acceleration upon Change of Control
- ISOs vs NSOs: Obligation to clearly explain tax implications to candidates

Education Mandate:
- Explain "409A price," "exercise price," and "tax impact" to all option holders
- Make clear that "grant ≠ asset"
```

### 4.4. Pay Transparency Compliance

- **Rule 52.033**: Comply with global Pay Transparency regulations

```
Pay Transparency Compliance Checklist:
□ Include salary ranges in job postings (CA SB 1162 / NY Pay Transparency Law)
□ Develop compliance plan for EU Pay Transparency Directive (effective 2026)
  - Remediation required if gender pay gap exceeds 5%
  - Periodic transparency reports on compensation structure
□ Establish internal salary band disclosure policy
□ Conduct annual Pay Equity analysis (see §14.5)
```

### 4.5. Benefits Design

- **Rule 52.034**: Design benefits across the following categories

| Category | Examples | Priority |
|:---------|:--------|:---------|
| **Health** | Medical, dental, mental health support | Required |
| **Financial** | Retirement plans, ESOP, student loan assistance | Required |
| **Flexibility** | Remote work, flex time | Required |
| **Learning** | Learning budget, books, conferences | Highly Recommended |
| **Wellbeing** | PTO, parental leave, sabbatical | Highly Recommended |
| **Perks** | Meals, commute, team building | Optional |

### 4.6. Global Compensation Strategy

- **Rule 52.035**: Define a global compensation strategy for distributed teams

```
3 Global Compensation Models:

1. Role-based Pay:
   - Same role, same pay regardless of location
   - Pros: Maximum fairness, uniform hiring competitiveness
   - Cons: Overpay risk in lower-cost markets
   - Recommended: High-mobility roles (e.g., engineering)

2. Geo-based Pay:
   - Compensation reflects local cost of living and labor market
   - Pros: Cost optimization, local market alignment
   - Cons: Fairness concerns, relocation adjustment issues
   - Recommended: Large-scale global operations (100+ employees)

3. Hybrid Pay:
   - Apply geographic multiplier (0.8-1.2) to a Role-based baseline
   - Pros: Balanced approach
   - Recommended: Mid-size (20-100) distributed teams

Selection Criteria:
- Org size < 50: Role-based recommended (fairness priority)
- Org size 50-200: Hybrid recommended
- Org size 200+: Geo-based with Equity Overlay

Currency Management:
- Local currency payment as default
- Review adjustments when FX fluctuation ≥ 10%
- Specify compensation currency in employment contracts
```

### 4.7. Variable Compensation Design

- **Rule 52.036**: Define design principles for variable compensation (bonuses and commissions)

```
Variable Compensation Framework:

1. Bonus:
   - Payout criteria: Company performance × Team performance × Individual performance (3 axes)
   - Allocation example: Company 40% / Team 30% / Individual 30%
   - Timing: Semi-annually (annual-only reduces motivational impact)
   - Maximum Cap: 30-50% of base salary (set by role and level)

2. Commission (Sales):
   - OTE (On-Target Earnings) = Base + Commission at 100% Quota
   - Base:Commission ratio: 60:40 (SaaS standard) to 50:50 (high-risk/high-reward)
   - Accelerator: Increased rate above quota (100-120%: 1.5x / 120%+: 2.0x)
   - Decelerator: Reduced commission below 50% attainment
   - Clawback: Commission returned if customer churns within 90 days

3. Sign-on Bonus:
   - Repayment: Full repayment for voluntary departure within 12 months
   - 6-12 months: Pro-rated repayment (monthly)
   - Timing: First paycheck (incentive to accept offer)

4. Phantom Equity / SAR:
   - Alternative for non-US entities where stock options are impractical
   - Phantom Stock: Cash payment equivalent to stock value
   - SAR (Stock Appreciation Rights): Cash payout of appreciation only
   - Vesting Schedule: Same as regular Equity (4 years/1-year cliff)
```

### 4.8. Retention Mechanisms

- **Rule 52.037**: Design retention mechanisms to prevent high-performer attrition

```
Retention Protocol:

1. Proactive Retention:
   - Regular Stay Interviews (§12.3)
   - Market compensation data refresh at least twice annually
   - Auto-alert for employees with Compa-Ratio < 0.9
   - Career path visualization and clear next-step articulation

2. Retention Bonus:
   - Target: Critical Talent with high Flight Risk
   - Amount: 10-25% of base salary (scaled by risk level)
   - Condition: 12-24 month continued tenure
   - M&A / Reorg: Add Stay Bonus of 6-12 months' compensation

3. Internal Counter Offer Guidelines:
   - Response flow upon resignation intent:
     a. Identify root cause (compensation / growth / culture / management)
     b. Consider Counter Offer only if compensation is the root cause
     c. Counter Offer is one-time only (repeated offers erode trust)
     d. Follow up carefully for 6 months after Counter Offer
   - Counter Offer rate cap: ≤ 20% of all resignation submissions

Prohibited:
❌ Pre-emptive raises for non-Flight-Risk employees (destroys fairness)
❌ Normalizing Counter Offers (encourages hostage-taking culture)
```

## Part V. Recruiting Process Design

### 5.1. Hiring Funnel Standard Design

- **Rule 52.040**: Design the hiring process using the following standard structure, completing it **within 4 weeks**

```
Sourcing → Screen → Phone Screen → Interview → Reference → Offer

Timeline targets:
Phone Screen: Scheduled within 3 days of contact
Interview: Scheduled within 1 week of Phone Screen
Offer: Delivered within 3 days of final interview

Prohibited: Hiring processes with more than 10 steps (primary cause of candidate drop-off)
```

### 5.2. Sourcing Diversification

- **Rule 52.041**: Leverage recruiting sources in the following priority order

| Priority | Source | Reason |
|:---------|:-------|:-------|
| 1 | **Internal Referral** | Highest quality, lowest cost, fastest |
| 2 | **LinkedIn Recruiter** | Large-scale search, passive candidates |
| 3 | **Niche Job Boards** | For specialized professionals |
| 4 | **Internal Mobility** | Leverage existing talent (§3.4) |
| 5 | **Recruiting Agency** | Speed-critical situations; high cost |
| 6 | **General Job Boards** | High volume but inconsistent quality |

### 5.3. Referral Program Design

- **Rule 52.042**: Build a Referral Program where all employees participate in hiring

```
Referral Program Design:
- Referral bonus: Paid when hire is confirmed and passes trial period
- Feedback loop: Share candidate outcomes with referring employees
- "Being a company people want to refer friends to" is the prerequisite
- Referral hiring ratio target: 30-50% of all hires

KPIs:
- Referral-to-Hire Rate
- Referral Quality Score (1-year performance of referral hires)
- Time-to-Fill via Referral
```

### 5.4. Recruiting KPIs

- **Rule 52.043**: Define and track the following recruiting KPIs monthly

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|:----|:-----------|:-------|
| **Time-to-Fill** | Days from req open to offer accepted | ≤ 30 days (IC) / ≤ 45 days (Manager) |
| **Time-to-Hire** | Days from first candidate contact to start date | ≤ 21 days |
| **Cost-per-Hire** | Total cost per hire | Below market benchmark |
| **Quality-of-Hire** | New hire 1-year performance rating | ≥ 70% rated Top 50%+ |
| **Offer Acceptance Rate** | % of offers accepted | ≥ 85% |
| **Source Effectiveness** | Cost-per-Quality-Hire by source | Analyzed quarterly |

## Part VI. Interview Process

### 6.1. Structured Interviews

- **Rule 52.050**: All hiring interviews must be **structured**. Unstructured conversational-style interviews are prohibited
- **Definition of Structured**: The same questions, in the same order, with pre-defined evaluation criteria applied to all candidates

### 6.2. Interview Type Mix

- **Rule 52.051**: Combine multiple interview types in each hiring process

| Type | Purpose | Example |
|:----|:--------|:--------|
| **Behavioral** | Predict future from past behavior | STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) |
| **Situational** | Test response to hypothetical scenarios | Case Interview |
| **Technical / Skills** | Direct assessment of expertise | Coding test / Design review |
| **Culture / Values** | Assess values and culture fit | Values Alignment Interview |
| **Reference** | Obtain third-party evaluation | Backchannel reference checks |

### 6.3. Technical Interview Design

- **Rule 52.052**: Technical interviews should evaluate with tasks resembling real work

```
Technical Interview Design Principles:
- Prefer Pair Programming / Live Coding over Whiteboard Coding
- Take-home assignments must be completable within 4 hours maximum
- Obtain candidate's consent before issuing take-home assignments
- Pre-define evaluation criteria (Rubric)

Evaluation Rubric Example (Technical Interview):
| Dimension | 1 (Fail) | 2 (Below Bar) | 3 (Pass) | 4 (Exceptional) |
|-----------|----------|---------------|----------|-----------------|
| Problem Understanding | Misunderstands reqs | Partial understanding | Correct understanding | Identifies implicit reqs |
| Design | No design | Basic design | Considers extensibility | Discusses tradeoffs |
| Code Quality | Non-functional | Works but low readability | High readability | Production quality |
| Communication | Silent | Minimal explanation | Verbalizes thinking | Active collaboration |
```

### 6.4. Interview Scorecard

- **Rule 52.053**: Use a standardized Scorecard for all interviews

```yaml
interview_scorecard:
  candidate_name: ""
  interviewer_name: ""
  interview_type: "behavioral | technical | culture | reference"
  date: ""
  overall_recommendation: "strong_hire | hire | lean_hire | lean_no_hire | no_hire | strong_no_hire"
  dimensions:
    - name: "Dimension 1"
      score: "1-4"
      evidence: "Specific observed facts"
    - name: "Dimension 2"
      score: "1-4"
      evidence: ""
  strengths: "Candidate strengths"
  concerns: "Concerns"
  notes: "Additional notes"
```

### 6.5. Debrief Protocol

- **Rule 52.054**: Conduct post-interview debriefs using the following rules

```
Debrief Rules:
1. All interviewers independently record their score before debrief (uninfluenced)
2. First state HIRE / NO HIRE / UNSURE, then discuss
3. A single strong "NO HIRE" warrants additional validation or rejection
4. Debrief agenda: not "Do we hire?" but "Does this person raise the bar?"

Prohibited:
❌ HiPPO Effect (everyone aligns with the most senior person's opinion)
❌ Hiring decision without debrief
❌ Participating in debrief without submitting Scorecard
```

### 6.6. Removing Unconscious Bias

- **Rule 52.055**: Structurally eliminate the following biases from hiring

| Bias Type | Prevention |
|:----------|:----------|
| **Similar-to-me Bias** | Pre-define evaluation criteria |
| **Halo Effect** | Separate evaluation axes to prevent one strength from inflating all scores |
| **First Impression Bias** | Consider resume-blind early screening |
| **Affinity Bias** | Ensure diversity among interviewers |
| **Confirmation Bias** | Include counter-evidence questions in Scorecard |
| **Horn Effect** | Mandate holistic assessment; don't let one weakness dominate |

### 6.7. Interviewer Training

- **Rule 52.056**: Only trained interviewers may conduct hiring interviews

```
Interviewer Training Requirements:
□ Structured Interview methodology
□ STAR method deep-dive techniques
□ Unconscious Bias recognition and prevention
□ Legally prohibited questions (age, marital status, religion, disability, etc.)
□ Scorecard completion methods
□ Importance of Candidate Experience

Certification:
- First-time: Shadow a Senior Interviewer (2+ sessions)
- Annual: Refresh training
```

## Part VII. Offer & Closing

### 7.1. Offer Timing and Design

- **Rule 52.060**: Send the offer within 3 business days of the final interview
  - **Verbal Offer First**: Call the candidate to share the offer intent and confirm acceptance before sending the letter
  - **Offer Letter Required Fields**: Title, compensation (base + variable), start date, stock option terms, probationary period, salary band information

### 7.2. Handling Undecided Candidates

- **Rule 52.061**: Protocol for candidates who are slow to decide

```
Steps to take:
1. Directly ask "What's making this a tough decision?" (don't assume)
2. Identify any competing offers and their key comparison factors
3. Respond to "post-joining risk concerns" with specific, concrete assurances
4. Remember the candidate's Push factor and validate those concerns
5. Team matching: Provide opportunities for the candidate to interact with future colleagues

Prohibited:
❌ Pressuring them to decide quickly (regret → early attrition)
❌ Over-promising to get the accept
❌ High-pressure deadlines like "We need your answer today"
```

### 7.3. Counter Offer Response

- **Rule 52.062**: Protocol when candidates receive a counter offer from their current employer

```
Counter Offer Response Protocol:
1. Return to "the essential reason they decided to leave"
2. Share counter offer statistics:
   - 50%+ of counter offer acceptors leave again within 18 months (industry data)
3. Help compare beyond compensation: career growth, culture, mission
4. Ultimately respect the candidate's autonomous decision (pushing destroys trust)
```

## Part VIII. Onboarding Design

### 8.1. Pre-boarding

- **Rule 52.070**: Design the period between offer acceptance and start date as Pre-boarding

```
Pre-boarding Checklist:
□ Send welcome message immediately upon acceptance
□ Welcome messages from team members
□ Pre-ship IT equipment (guarantee delivery before start date)
□ Share pre-reading materials (product overview, culture guide)
□ Share Day 1 schedule
□ Introduce Buddy in advance
```

### 8.2. 90-Day Onboarding Program

- **Rule 52.071**: Design employee onboarding as a structured 90-day program

```
Day 1-7 (Day 1 Experience):
□ All tools set up on Day 1 (mandatory)
□ Company / product / culture orientation
□ 1:1 with every team member (even 15 minutes works)
□ Experience a first "win" (small, early contribution)
□ Security & compliance training

Week 2-4 (Role Deep Dive):
□ Deep learning of assigned domain
□ Shadowing (observe senior peers)
□ Set 30/60/90-day goals (agreed with manager)
□ Complete first Pull Request or Deliverable

Month 2-3 (Onboarding to Ownership):
□ First independent project
□ Mid-point check-in (reflection with manager)
□ Culture fit confirmation (bidirectional)
□ 90-day review: Onboarding completion assessment
```

### 8.3. Buddy Program

- **Rule 52.072**: Assign every new hire a "Buddy" (a non-manager peer)
  - Buddy's role: Guide for culture and tacit knowledge—not technical questions
  - Duration: First 90 days
  - Buddy workload: Keep Buddy duties within 10% of regular workload

### 8.4. Onboarding KPIs

- **Rule 52.073**: Measure onboarding success with the following metrics

| KPI | Definition | Target |
|:----|:-----------|:-------|
| **Time-to-Productivity** | Time until initial independent contribution | ≤ 60 days |
| **Onboarding NPS** | New hire onboarding satisfaction | ≥ 70 |
| **90-Day Retention** | Turnover within first 90 days | < 5% |
| **Manager Satisfaction** | Manager's assessment of new hire | ≥ 3.5/5.0 |

### 8.5. Remote Onboarding

- **Rule 52.074**: Add the following for remote onboarding

```
Remote Onboarding Additions:
□ Virtual office tour / team introduction video
□ Async communication tools orientation guide
□ Daily 30-minute manager check-in during first week
□ Virtual lunch / coffee chats (2+ per week)
□ Intentionally create a "safe to ask questions" environment (dedicated Slack channel, etc.)
```

## Part IX. Performance Management

### 9.1. Continuous Performance Management

- **Rule 52.080**: Performance management is a **continuous process**, not an annual event

```
3 Pillars of Continuous PM:
1. Weekly 1:1: Manager-report weekly dialogue (§9.3)
2. Quarterly Check-in: OKR progress + career development quarterly review
3. Annual/Bi-annual Review: Formal evaluation, compensation, promotion decisions (§9.5)

Prohibited:
❌ Surprises at year-end review (issues not raised in prior 1:1s)
```

### 9.2. OKR Design

- **Rule 52.081**: Use OKRs for team goal management

```
OKR Design Principles:
- Objective: Qualitative, emotionally motivating direction (Why, not What)
- Key Result: Quantitative, measurable, time-bound
- Number: 2-5 Key Results per Objective
- Difficulty: 70% achievement = "great" (Moonshots)
- Transparency: All OKRs publicly visible company-wide
- Update Cadence: Set quarterly, review monthly

Prohibited:
❌ Key Results describing activities (e.g., "Launch X") — define outcomes, not tasks
❌ Key Results with zero stretch (no ambition)
❌ Using OKRs as a substitute for MBO / KPIs (OKRs are aspirational)
```

### 9.3. 1:1 Meeting Standards

- **Rule 52.082**: 1:1s between manager and direct report must occur at minimum weekly for 30 minutes

```
1:1 Agenda Structure:
1. Employee's current state / concerns (15 min)
   ← Manager is in Listening Mode
2. Blockers / areas needing support (10 min)
3. Goals / career development touchpoint (5 min)

Prohibited:
❌ Converting 1:1s into status update meetings (that's a separate meeting)
❌ Manager dominates the conversation
❌ Canceling 1:1s two weeks in a row (a serious management dysfunction signal)
```

### 9.4. Skip-level 1:1

- **Rule 52.083**: Manager's manager (skip-level) holds direct 1:1s with each team member at least once per quarter
  - Purpose: Validate management quality, gauge organizational mood, provide career guidance
  - Proactively lower the psychological barrier of "going over the manager's head"

### 9.5. Performance Review Protocol

- **Rule 52.084**: Conduct performance reviews bi-annually, evaluating on 3 dimensions

```
3 Evaluation Dimensions:
1. What (Results): OKR / goal achievement
2. How (Behavior): Aligned with company values and culture?
3. Potential: Could this person excel at the next level role?

Prohibited:
❌ Surprises (issues should have been raised in prior 1:1s)
❌ Year-end only reviews (doesn't replace continuous feedback)
❌ Recency Bias (evaluating based only on recent impressions)
```

### 9.6. Calibration

- **Rule 52.085**: Conduct Calibration sessions after performance reviews

```
Calibration Protocol:
- Purpose: Correct variance in evaluation standards across managers
- Participants: Managers of same-level employees + HR
- Method: Each manager explains evaluation rationale; cross-review
- Criteria: Ensure employees at the same level with same results receive consistent ratings
- Output: Final ratings, compensation adjustments, promotion decisions

Prohibited:
❌ Compensation/promotion decisions without Calibration
❌ Forced Ranking / Stack Ranking
```

### 9.7. PIP (Performance Improvement Plan)

- **Rule 52.086**: PIP protocol for underperforming employees

```
PIP Design Principles:
1. Clearly communicate that PIP is an "improvement opportunity," not a "termination precursor"
2. Define improvement goals using SMART criteria
3. Duration: 30-90 days (adjusted by role and level)
4. Weekly check-ins: Manager monitors progress and provides support
5. PIP outcomes are threefold: Improvement complete / Extension / Separation (last resort)

Prerequisites:
- Sufficient feedback must have been delivered in 1:1s before PIP initiation
- PIP content must be reviewed by HR and Legal
- The PIP itself must never be a surprise
```

### 9.8. 360-Degree Feedback Protocol

- **Rule 52.087**: Promote self-awareness and leadership development through multi-rater feedback

```
360-Degree Feedback Design:

Timing:
- Annual: All managers (mandatory)
- Annual: IC Senior level and above (recommended)
- Promotion reviews: All candidates (mandatory)

Rater Selection Protocol:
- Manager (Direct Manager): 1
- Peers: 3-5 (nominated by subject + additions by manager)
- Direct Reports: All (for managers)
- Cross-functional: 1-2 (collaborators from other teams)
- External Stakeholders: Optional (for client-facing roles)

Anonymity Design:
- Individual rater responses are aggregated anonymously
- Only category-level (manager / peers / reports) summaries disclosed to subject
- If direct reports < 3, merge reports category into "peers" to protect anonymity

Utilization Requirements:
- 360 FB results must directly feed into IDP (§10.2)
- Use as "How axis" data for promotion decisions (§9.5)
- Using 360 FB punitively is prohibited
```

### 9.9. Real-time Feedback & CFR

- **Rule 52.088**: Build a real-time feedback culture independent of annual reviews

```
CFR (Conversations, Feedback, Recognition) Model:

1. Conversations:
   - Conduct 1:1s weekly (§9.3 enhancement)
   - Agenda: Include career and wellbeing beyond work progress
   - Manager Listen:Talk ratio target = 70:30

2. Feedback:
   - 72-hour rule: Deliver feedback within 72 hours of observing behavior
   - SBI framework: Situation → Behavior → Impact
   - Feedback Ratio: Target positive:constructive = 5:1
   - micro-feedback: Encourage real-time praise via Slack/Teams

3. Recognition:
   - Normalize Peer-to-Peer Recognition (integrate with §12.5)
   - Tie recognition to "specific behavior + Values alignment"
   - Encourage recognition in public channels (increase visibility)

Anti-Patterns:
❌ Raising issues for the first time at annual review (§9.1 Continuous PM violation)
❌ Conflating feedback with evaluation (feedback is growth support)
❌ Biasing toward only positive or only negative feedback
```

### 12.6. Employee Wellbeing & Burnout Prevention

- **Rule 52.115**: Organizationally protect employee wellbeing and prevent burnout

```
Burnout Definition (WHO ICD-11):
- Element 1: Emotional Exhaustion
- Element 2: Depersonalization (cynical attitudes)
- Element 3: Reduced Personal Accomplishment

Burnout Prevention Protocol:

1. Early Detection:
   - Include burnout indicators in Pulse Surveys (§12.2)
   - "Have you felt helpless in the past 2 weeks?"
   - "Is your workload manageable?"
   - Alert manager if scores decline for 2 consecutive quarters

2. Preventive Measures:
   - Visualize Workload Capacity (project count × urgency map per member)
   - Ensure at least 1 "No Meeting Day" per week
   - Monitor PTO utilization rate (target: ≥ 80%)
   - Burnout recognition training for managers

3. Mental Health First Aider Program:
   - Deploy at least 1 trained employee per team
   - Role: Initial response, bridge to professionals, confidentiality
   - Certification renewal: Annual (reflecting latest mental health knowledge)

4. EAP (Employee Assistance Program):
   - Provide professional counseling services to all employees
   - Free, fully anonymous, 24/7 availability
   - Scope: Mental health, family issues, financial counseling, legal counseling
   - Utilization KPI: ≥ 5% (if too low, implement awareness campaigns)

5. Sabbatical Program:
   - Eligibility: 5+ years of tenure
   - Duration: 4-12 weeks (paid or half-paid)
   - Return guarantee: Return to same or equivalent position

6. Compassionate Leave:
   - Death of immediate family: 5-10 business days
   - Serious family illness: Flexible work arrangements (remote/reduced hours)
   - Miscarriage/stillbirth: Adequate leave period guaranteed
```

### 12.7. Right to Disconnect

- **Rule 52.116**: Define policies protecting employees from after-hours communication

```
Right to Disconnect Policy:

Legal Context:
- EU Right to Disconnect Directive (under discussion, as of 2026)
- France: El Khomri Law (disconnect agreement obligation for 50+ employee companies)
- Portugal & Ireland: Legislated

Operational Rules:
1. Core Hours Definition: Clearly define team core hours (e.g., 10:00-16:00 local time)
2. After-hours Policy: Prohibit expectation of immediate response outside core hours
3. Send Later Recommended: Use scheduled send for messages composed outside hours
4. Deep Work Protection: Ensure minimum 2 hours of uninterrupted focus time daily
5. Urgency Protocol: Use phone only for true emergencies (Slack/Email are not urgent channels)

KPIs:
- Off-hours message frequency: Track message send rate outside core hours
- Target: Off-hours sends ≤ 10%
- On trend deterioration: Alert managers and develop improvement plans

Prohibited:
❌ Including after-hours message response speed in evaluation criteria
❌ Cultivating an "always available" expectation culture
```

## Part XV. Organization Design Principles

### 15.1. Conway's Law Adherence

- **Rule 52.140**: Apply Conway's Law: "Organizations design systems that mirror their communication structure." Design team structures with product architecture in mind

### 15.2. Inverse Conway Maneuver

- **Rule 52.141**: Define the desired architecture first, then design the org structure to match (Inverse Conway Maneuver)

```
Inverse Conway Maneuver Steps:
1. Design the ideal product architecture
2. Derive the team structure that enables that architecture
3. Set boundaries that minimize cross-team communication costs
4. Define inter-team APIs (interaction modes)

Prohibited:
❌ Constraining architecture based on existing org structure
```

### 15.3. Team Topology

- **Rule 52.142**: Design team structures using the 4 types from Team Topologies (Skelton & Pais)

| Type | Purpose | Example |
|:-----|:--------|:--------|
| **Stream-Aligned** | Continuous delivery of business value | Feature teams (Checkout team, etc.) |
| **Enabling** | Raise capabilities of other teams | Platform Engineering |
| **Complicated Subsystem** | Specialized, complex functionality | Machine Learning team |
| **Platform** | Internal platform provision | DevOps / Infrastructure |

### 15.4. Team Interaction Modes

- **Rule 52.143**: Explicitly define Team Topologies' 3 interaction modes

```
Interaction Modes:
1. Collaboration: Two teams work closely to solve a new challenge (temporary)
2. X-as-a-Service: One team provides APIs/platform as a service to others
3. Facilitating: Enabling team helps other teams build capabilities (temporary)

Principles:
- Set a default interaction mode for every team pair
- Collaboration mode is temporary (6-12 weeks); don't let it become permanent
```

### 15.5. Cognitive Load Management

- **Rule 52.144**: Manage team cognitive load and prevent overload

```
3 Types of Cognitive Load:
1. Intrinsic: Domain-inherent complexity
2. Extraneous: Unnecessary complexity from tools, processes, environment
3. Germane: Complexity necessary for learning and growth

Management Principles:
- Minimize Extraneous Load (tool consolidation, process automation)
- If a team's cognitive load is excessive, reduce scope or split the team
- Set upper limits on domains/services a single team owns
```

### 15.6. Span of Control

- **Rule 52.145**: A manager's direct reports should not exceed **5-8 people**
  - If direct IC management exceeds 8: Hire a new Manager
  - Manager of Managers (department head): Up to 5 direct managers

### 15.7. Organizational Scaling Thresholds

- **Rule 52.146**: Define structural change thresholds based on organization size

```
Scaling Guidelines:
- ~8 people: Everyone collaborates flat (no management needed)
- ~15 people: First manager hire (Dunbar's Number: 15 = trust limit)
- ~50 people: Hire dedicated HR; begin formalizing processes
- ~150 people: Departmentalize; introduce middle management (Dunbar's Number: 150)
- ~500 people: VP/Director tier; establish specialized staff functions
- ~1000+: Build a full People Operations team
```

## Part XVII. Organizational Culture Design & Measurement

### 17.1. Culture Code Definition

```
Culture Code Definition Process:

```

### 17.2. Psychological Safety

- **Rule 52.161**: Apply Amy Edmondson's psychological safety framework organization-wide

```
4 Stages of Psychological Safety:
1. Inclusion Safety: "I feel I belong here"
2. Learner Safety: "It's safe to ask questions"
3. Contributor Safety: "It's safe to share ideas"
4. Challenger Safety: "It's safe to challenge the status quo"

Measurement:
- Include psychological safety questions in Pulse Survey
  - "Do you feel safe admitting mistakes within your team?"
  - "Is risk-taking encouraged?"
  - "Are different opinions welcomed?"
- Target: Psychological Safety score ≥ 4.0/5.0

Promotion Tactics:
- Leaders share their own failures openly
- Implement Blameless Post-mortems
- Create mechanisms for voicing "hard truths" (anonymous Q&A, suggestion box)
```

### 17.3. Radical Candor Culture

- **Rule 52.162**: Use Kim Scott's Radical Candor as the foundation for feedback culture

```
2 Axes of Radical Candor:
1. Care Personally
2. Challenge Directly

┌──────────────────────────────┐
│ Care + No Challenge          │ Care + Challenge              │
│ = Ruinous Empathy            │ = Radical Candor ★            │
├──────────────────────────────┤
│ No Care + No Challenge       │ No Care + Challenge           │
│ = Manipulative Insincerity   │ = Obnoxious Aggression        │
└──────────────────────────────┘

Implementation:
- Deliver feedback within 72 hours (while memory is fresh)
- Feedback structure: SBI (Situation → Behavior → Impact)
- Not "You are X" but "In [situation], [behavior] occurred, leading to [impact]"
```

### 17.4. Change Management

- **Rule 52.163**: Systematically manage organizational change and prevent change fatigue

```
ADKAR Model (Change Management Framework):

1. Awareness: Everyone understands why change is needed
2. Desire: Form motivation to participate in change
3. Knowledge: Learn new behaviors and processes post-change
4. Ability: Become capable of executing new behaviors
5. Reinforcement: Embed change and prevent regression

Kotter 8-Step Model:
1. Create a sense of urgency
2. Build a guiding coalition
3. Form a strategic vision
4. Communicate the vision organization-wide
5. Remove barriers to action
6. Generate short-term wins
7. Consolidate gains to drive further change
8. Anchor new approaches in the culture

Change Readiness Assessment:
- Evaluate organizational readiness before change
- Axes: Leadership support / Frontline change resilience / Past change track record
- Invest additional support and communication in low-score areas

Change Fatigue Management:
- Set upper limit on concurrent change initiatives (recommended: ≤ 2 per team)
- Visualize change "outcomes" to show efforts are paying off
- Ensure "stability periods" between change cycles
```

### 17.5. Internal Communication Standards

- **Rule 52.164**: Define organizational information flow standards to minimize information asymmetry

```
Internal Communication Framework:

1. All-hands / Town Hall:
   - Frequency: Monthly (company-wide) + Quarterly (department-level)
   - Structure: CEO Update → KPI Share → Q&A (including anonymous questions)
   - Recording mandatory: For employees unable to attend

2. Information Tiering:
   - L1 (Public): Company vision, culture, general performance
   - L2 (Internal only): Detailed financials, product roadmap
   - L3 (Leadership only): M&A considerations, personnel planning
   - L4 (Confidential): Layoff plans, legal matters

3. Bad News Protocol:
   - Principles: Immediate × Transparent × Action Plan
   - Never underreport bad news (destroys trust)
   - Communicate using: "What happened → Why it happened → What we're doing" structure

4. Internal Newsletter / Digest:
   - Weekly digest: Product updates, new hire introductions, internal events
   - Monthly newsletter: Strategy updates, performance highlights, employee spotlights
   - Bidirectional: Include comment and reaction capabilities
```
