# 140. User Support & Customer Success

## 1. Support Philosophy ("Empathy & Efficiency")
*   **Empathy First**:
    *   **Rule**: Start every reply by empathizing with the user's problem. Do not just use mechanical templates; add a "Human Touch".
    *   **Goal**: Turn dissatisfied users into fans through the support experience.
*   **Self-Service First**:
    *   **FAQ**: Constantly update the FAQ so users can solve issues before contacting support.
    *   **AI Bots**: Use AI chatbots for first-line response to aim for instant resolution, but provide a smooth "Human Handover" if unresolved.

## 2. Inquiry Handling
*   **SLA (Service Level Agreement)**:
    *   **First Response**: Within **2 hours** during business hours, and **24 hours** otherwise.
    *   **Resolution**: Aim for full resolution within **48 hours**, excluding complex technical issues.
*   **Tooling**:
    *   **Centralization**: Manage inquiries from Email, Chat, and SNS in one place (e.g., Zendesk, Intercom). Zero missed inquiries.
    *   **Templates**: Use high-quality templates for frequent questions, but always customize them to the situation.

## 3. FAQ & Knowledge Base
*   **Dynamic Updates**:
    *   **Rule**: If the same question comes **3 times**, add it to the FAQ or improve the product UI to prevent the question.
    *   **Searchability**: Tag with synonyms and natural language so users can find answers using their own words.
*   **Clarity**:
    *   Avoid jargon. Use screenshots and GIF videos extensively to explain things simply.

## 4. Feedback Loop
*   **Voice of Customer (VoC)**:
    *   Aggregate "User Voices" (requests, complaints) weekly and feed them back to the Product Team.
    *   **Bug Reports**: Verify bug reports with top priority, and once fixed, personally report back to the user to thank them.
*   **NPS (Net Promoter Score)**:
    *   Measure service recommendation score regularly. Welcome critical feedback as a treasure trove for improvement.

## 5. Onboarding & Education
*   **Path to Success**:
    *   Minimize the time from registration to the "Aha! Moment" (first success experience).
    *   Tutorials should teach "Value" (what they can become), not just "Operations" (how to use).
