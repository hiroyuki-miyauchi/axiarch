# 140. User Support & Customer Success

## 1. Support Philosophy ("Empathy & Efficiency")
*   **Empathy First**:
    *   **Rule**: Start every reply by "empathizing with the user's trouble". Do not end with just mechanical templates; always add a Human Touch.
    *   **Goal**: Aim to turn dissatisfied users into "Fans" through the support experience.
*   **Self-Service First**:
    *   **FAQ Enrichment**: Constantly update FAQs (Frequently Asked Questions) so users can solve issues before inquiring.
    *   **AI Bot**: Use AI chat bots for initial responses to aim for immediate resolution, but switch smoothly to human support (Human Handover) if resolution is not possible.

## 2. Proactive Success
*   **Churn Prediction**:
    *   **Data-Driven**: Do not wait for contact from users. Monitor behavioral data (decrease in login frequency, non-use of key features) and automatically follow up (email, push notification) when churn risk increases.
*   **Support-Driven Development**:
    *   **Issue Linking**: Link support inquiries directly to GitHub Issues. Automate the rule "If the same inquiry comes 3 times, raise priority as a development task".

## 3. Inquiry Handling
*   **SLA (Service Level Agreement)**:
    *   **First Response Time**: Guarantee an initial reply within **2 hours** during business hours on weekdays, and within **24 hours** otherwise.
    *   **Resolution Time**: Aim for complete resolution within **48 hours**, excluding complex technical issues.
*   **Tooling**:
    *   **Centralized Management**: Centralize inquiries from email, chat, and SNS using tools like Zendesk or Intercom. Eliminate oversight (leakage).
    *   **Template Usage**: Prepare high-quality templates for frequent questions, but always customize them according to the situation.

## 4. FAQ & Knowledge Base
*   **Dynamic Updates**:
    *   **Rule**: If the same question comes "3 times", strictly add it to the FAQ or improve the product UI to prevent the question from arising.
    *   **Searchability**: Tag with synonyms and natural language so that users can hit results even when searching in their own words.
*   **Clarity**:
    *   Avoid technical jargon and use plenty of screenshots and GIF videos to explain in a way anyone can understand.

## 5. Feedback Loop
*   **Voice of Customer**:
    *   Aggregate "User Voices (Requests, Complaints)" obtained through support weekly and feed them back to the Development Team (Product Team).
    *   **Bug Reports**: Verify bug reports from users with top priority, and once fixed, always report individually to the user to express gratitude.
*   **NPS (Net Promoter Score)**:
    *   Conduct NPS surveys regularly to measure service recommendation levels. Welcome critical opinions as a treasure trove for improvement.

## 6. Onboarding & Education
*   **Path to Success**:
    *   Minimize the time from user registration to experiencing the "First Success (Aha! Moment)".
    *   Tutorials should convey "What you will be able to do (Value)" rather than "How to operate".
